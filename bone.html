bone handicraft products

Bone carving dates back to pre-historic era, across all civilizations. This involves cutting and carving of bone & horn to create ornate or utilitarian articles. After the ban of ivory, bones & horns of naturally deceased large animals such as camel & buffalo are the basic raw materials for this craft. The art forms include carving, filigree & metal inlay work on bone & horn. These are also mixed with wood, MDF, brass etc to create more versatile & bigger product range. The final products include ornate jewelry, boxes & frames, handles & knobs, table ware, decorative sculptures, and many other home d√©cor accessories. The process involves cutting of bone pieces in required shape & size, cleaning & disinfecting.
